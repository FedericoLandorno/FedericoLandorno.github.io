Project Eir

Visualization of world clinical trials to understand which ones are most studied and compare them.

Developer: HTML, CSS, JS (jQuery & D3)
Course: Information Visualization

About
Project Eir
Information Visualization includes techniques and principles for visualization of information with examples, demonstrations and lab assignments within different applications.
Managing and showing more than 300,000 clinical trials for researchers is a real problem.
This project aims to facilitate this problem by displaying information to make it easy to find details, give a good overview, explore clinical tests, and also filter, brush, and search for data.
The outcome of the course gave me the opportunity to improve different skills. We learned to retrieve data from in different ways and blend them together, We increased our knowledge in the use of D3.js, and more importantly apply information visualization theory along with data categorization.

Memory AR

The Memory card game brought in AR where the user will have to remember sounds and vibrations instead of images.

Developer: Unity, C#, Augmented Reality
Course: Multimodal Interaction

About
Memory AR
Multimodal interaction and interfaces is focused on the interaction between humans and computers, using interfaces that employ several modalities and human senses, such as speech, gestures and touch.
The purpose of the Memory AR project was to compare two different modalities, which are not commonly found in a typical Memory game. For once we wanted to enable visually impaired people to play a game of memory. In addition to that we wanted to compare the capability to perceive and remember different modalities. In this paper we show how users experience the new Memory AR, which play sound or vibration patterns instead of showing images. Furthermore, how good the participants are at perceiving the sound and vibration patterns. We also aim to investigate if a redundant implementation of sound and vibration improved the recognition and remembering rate and perhaps made the game more engaging.

Soundscape of urban transport

Design of the soundscape of the future for the metro and the streets of Stockholm.

Interaction and Sound Designer
Course: Sound in Interaction

About
Soundscape of urban transport
Sound in Interaction offers a multi-disciplinary overview of the principles underlying the use of sound in interaction between users and machines.
The soundscapes of environments of urban transportation means are oftentimes overlooked by frequent visitors. This paper aims to bring a broadened acoustic awareness to one such environment, namely the Stockholm metro and adjacent urban soundscape. While aiming to provide a sensitizing experience, users are invited to move through a small scale physical model of the Stockholm metro and urban environment, made available by the use of a motion capture system, and experience its surrounding present and imagined future sounds. With the larger goal of improving the sonic interaction experience, particularly in regards to the future soundscape, the choice of sounds was continuously iterated and discussed with a focus on their added value and mapping of physical dimensions to auditory ones. Through preference estimation with one test participant, an urban planner and public transport enthusiast, the model did bring acoustic awareness as intended. However, with the present and future sound being rather similar, the future rendering could have benefitted from using sounds less conforming to the present soundscape.
